Maurya Empire

Establishment of the Maurya Empire

The Maurya Empire was founded by Chandragupta Maurya around 322 BCE. He overthrew the Nanda dynasty, establishing the Mauryan rule over a vast territory in the Indian subcontinent. Chandragupta's advisor, Chanakya, played a crucial role in the empire's formation.

Rulers of the Maurya Empire

The Maurya Empire had notable rulers who contributed to its expansion and governance:

1. Chandragupta Maurya (c. 322 – 298 BCE)

Chandragupta was the founder of the Maurya Empire. He successfully conquered the Magadha region and later expanded his empire, encompassing a significant portion of North India. His alliance with Chanakya, also known as Kautilya, played a pivotal role in his military successes.

2. Bindusara (c. 298 – 273 BCE)

Bindusara, the son of Chandragupta, continued the expansion of the Maurya Empire. His reign saw the incorporation of additional territories into the empire, extending its influence to the Deccan region.
